Swarm Robotics Research

I have been an undergraduate research assistant with the Rubenstein Lab since my first year at Northwestern. During this time, I have designed electronics mounts, built robots and coded the operating system infrastructure.

CAD 3D Printing Testing Python/C Simulation Data Management

Building quadrotor electronics housing components

One of my first projects was to design housing for the electrical components onboard the robot.

I went through many iterations of spacer designs (e.g. snap fit spacers) and board designs. All of my iterations were tested via 3D printing on Bambu/Ultimaker printers. I learned about tolerances and the obstacles in translating a CAD design into reality based on the manufacturing method involved.

Tuning PID/LQR control

After gaining experience building and assembling the robots, I tuned the PID and LQR versions of the robot position control. I conducted flight experiments in the OptiTrack motion capture arena to observe and record the tests. After tuning, the position error reduced to about 2cm during flight.

Ongoing - Landing sequence behavior

My latest work involves programming for the landing and recharging sequence when there is a larger swarm. I am identifying which landing stations are occupied and available, and sending this information to the robots to make their decision on where to land for a battery swap.
