Modular Fin Clamp System

I took the lead on the fin can design for the NASA Student Launch rocketry competition as part of NUSTARS.

OnShape CAD Tensile Testing Workshop Tools Mechanical Design Presentation Teamwork

Research and ideation

Our research phase consisted of reviewing past fin system attempts by NUSTARS as well as other collegiate team designs. The key considerations for our design were weight reduction and durability under in-flight forces.

Prototyping and testing

After narrowing down to a modular fin clamp design, my focus shifted to optimizing the number of fasteners and material infill pattern properties of the 3D printed clamps. For conducting material infill testing, we 3D printed several patterned ABS clamps and used the UTM for tensile testing. This revealed that rectilinear infill was the more promising pattern. Other tests included fin deflection and bending when in the built assembly.

Design review with NASA panel

Our design was evaluated and presented to the competition judges over several milestones. I presented our fin system design during the Critical Design Review to a panel of engineers and experts, receiving positive feedback.

Manufacturing and launch

The fiberglass fins and 3D printed clamp structure were integrated into our final competition rocket, which was launched several times over the academic year. Our rocket, Delphi, flew its final flight in Huntsville, Alabama in May 2025!
