Level 2 High Power Rocketry

In spring 2025, I built and launched Symphony, my own level 2 high power rocket, to obtain my NAR certification.

OnShape CAD OpenRocket Manufacturing

Design - CAD

I based my CAD model off the Wildman Drago 4 kit. I created a model using OnShape to verify the lengths and fit of the launch vehicle components. I also designed my custom avionics sled for the flight computers, which was 3D printed from PLA.

Design - OpenRocket

I used OpenRocket to simulate the flight (J450 motor) and recovery based on my chosen 48" parachute and drogue shock cord. My recovery method was head-end deployment of the parachute.

Ground testing

I needed to size my ejection charges using hand calculations. I evaluated the force required to shear the shear pins at the nose cone and booster section, then determined how many grams of black powder I needed to create sufficient pressure.

I found that my ejection charges required 3 grams of black powder. To verify this would create successful separation, I conducted a ground test that simulated apogee and main deployment.

Launch!

On June 7th, I launched Symphony at the Richard Bong State Recreation park in Wisconsin. The launch and recovery were successful, so I was Level 2 certified!

Afterwards, I retrieved the flight data from the onboard RRC3 flight computers, which showed a total apogee of 3490ft and parachute deployment at 600ft.
